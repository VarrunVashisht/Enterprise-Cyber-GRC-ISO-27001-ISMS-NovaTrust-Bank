# NovaTrust Bank
## Cybersecurity Governance Program
### Module 13 – Incident Response & Cyber Attack Simulation

---

# 1. Introduction

Cybersecurity incidents are inevitable in modern digital environments. Financial institutions must therefore maintain strong incident response capabilities to detect, contain, and recover from cyber attacks.

NovaTrust Bank maintains a structured incident response program designed to minimize operational disruption, protect customer data, and maintain regulatory compliance.

This section simulates a ransomware attack scenario to demonstrate how NovaTrust Bank detects and responds to cybersecurity incidents.

---

# 2. Incident Response Objectives

The incident response program at NovaTrust Bank is designed to achieve the following objectives:

• detect cybersecurity incidents quickly  
• contain threats before they spread across systems  
• investigate the root cause of incidents  
• restore normal operations as quickly as possible  
• report incidents to leadership and regulators when required

These objectives support operational resilience within the bank.

---

# 3. Incident Response Team Structure

NovaTrust Bank maintains a dedicated incident response team responsible for managing cybersecurity incidents.

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|---------------|
| CISO | Overall incident leadership and executive reporting |
| Security Operations Team | Detect and investigate incidents |
| IT Infrastructure Team | Contain and restore affected systems |
| Legal & Compliance Team | Regulatory reporting and legal assessment |
| Communications Team | Customer and public communication |

This multidisciplinary team ensures that incidents are handled effectively across technical and business domains.

---

# 4. Incident Response Lifecycle

NovaTrust Bank follows a structured incident response lifecycle.

The lifecycle includes the following stages:

1. Detection  
2. Analysis  
3. Containment  
4. Eradication  
5. Recovery  
6. Lessons Learned

This structured approach ensures that incidents are handled consistently and efficiently.

---

# 5. Simulated Cyber Attack Scenario

The following scenario demonstrates how NovaTrust Bank responds to a ransomware attack targeting its internal network.

## Attack Scenario

A phishing email is sent to several employees within the bank. One employee unknowingly downloads a malicious attachment that installs ransomware malware on the workstation.

The malware attempts to spread laterally across the network and encrypt file servers containing internal banking data.

---

# 6. Detection Phase

NovaTrust Bank's Security Operations Center detects suspicious activity through endpoint monitoring tools.

Indicators detected include:

• abnormal file encryption activity  
• unusual network connections from the compromised device  
• elevated system privileges used by malware

The SOC generates a high-severity security alert indicating potential ransomware activity.

---

# 7. Analysis Phase

SOC analysts investigate the alert by reviewing system logs and endpoint activity.

Investigation reveals:

• malware execution on an employee workstation  
• attempted lateral movement across internal systems  
• suspicious outbound communication with external command-and-control servers

Based on this analysis, the event is confirmed as a ransomware attack.

---

# 8. Containment Phase

The incident response team immediately initiates containment procedures.

Containment actions include:

• isolating the infected workstation from the network  
• disabling compromised user accounts  
• blocking malicious IP addresses and domains  
• restricting access to affected network segments

These actions prevent the ransomware from spreading further across the bank's infrastructure.

---

# 9. Eradication Phase

After containment, the incident response team removes the malware from affected systems.

Actions include:

• removing malicious files and processes  
• scanning systems for additional infections  
• patching exploited vulnerabilities  
• resetting affected user credentials

These actions eliminate the threat from the network.

---

# 10. Recovery Phase

Once the malware is removed, the bank restores affected systems using secure backups.

Recovery activities include:

• restoring encrypted files from backup systems  
• validating system integrity  
• reconnecting systems to the network  
• monitoring systems for recurring malicious activity

This ensures that banking operations can resume safely.

---

# 11. Lessons Learned

After the incident is resolved, NovaTrust Bank conducts a post-incident review.

Key review questions include:

• how the attack occurred  
• which controls were effective  
• where security improvements are needed  
• how detection capabilities can be improved

The results of the review help strengthen the bank's cybersecurity defenses.

---

# 12. Regulatory Reporting

Financial institutions may be required to report significant cybersecurity incidents to regulators.

NovaTrust Bank's legal and compliance teams evaluate whether regulatory reporting is required based on incident severity.

Regulatory reporting ensures transparency and compliance with financial sector regulations.

---

# 13. Continuous Improvement of Incident Response

NovaTrust Bank continuously improves its incident response capabilities through:

• incident response exercises  
• threat simulation exercises  
• security training programs  
• regular updates to response procedures

These improvements ensure that the organization remains prepared for future cyber incidents.

---

# 14. Importance of Incident Response Preparedness

Preparedness for cybersecurity incidents is essential for maintaining operational resilience in financial institutions.

By maintaining strong incident response capabilities, NovaTrust Bank can:

• limit the impact of cyber attacks  
• protect customer financial information  
• maintain trust in digital banking services  
• meet regulatory expectations
