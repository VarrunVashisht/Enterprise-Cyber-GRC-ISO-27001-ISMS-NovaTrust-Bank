# NovaTrust Bank
## Cybersecurity Governance Program
### Module 15 – Third-Party Risk Management (TPRM)

---

# 1. Introduction

Third-party service providers play a critical role in the operation of modern financial institutions. NovaTrust Bank relies on external vendors for cloud infrastructure, payment processing, software platforms, and operational support services.

While these partnerships enable digital banking innovation, they also introduce cybersecurity risks. A security weakness within a vendor's environment could expose NovaTrust Bank to supply chain attacks, data breaches, or operational disruptions.

The Third-Party Risk Management (TPRM) program ensures that NovaTrust Bank identifies, evaluates, and manages cybersecurity risks associated with third-party service providers.

---

# 2. Objectives of Third-Party Risk Management

The objectives of NovaTrust Bank's TPRM program include:

• identifying third-party cybersecurity risks  
• ensuring vendors maintain appropriate security controls  
• protecting customer financial data shared with vendors  
• reducing supply chain cyber risks  
• maintaining regulatory compliance

These objectives ensure that external partnerships do not compromise the bank's cybersecurity posture.

---

# 3. Third-Party Vendor Categories

NovaTrust Bank works with various types of external vendors.

| Vendor Category | Example Services |
|----------------|-----------------|
| Cloud Providers | Cloud hosting infrastructure |
| Payment Processors | Payment transaction services |
| Software Vendors | Banking software platforms |
| Fintech Partners | Digital financial services |
| Managed IT Providers | Infrastructure support and monitoring |

Each vendor category introduces different levels of cybersecurity risk.

---

# 4. Third-Party Risk Assessment Process

Before engaging with any vendor, NovaTrust Bank performs a third-party cybersecurity risk assessment.

The assessment process includes:

1. Vendor identification  
2. Security risk evaluation  
3. Security control review  
4. contract security requirements  
5. approval by risk management teams

This evaluation ensures that vendors meet NovaTrust Bank's security standards before gaining access to sensitive systems or data.

---

# 5. Vendor Risk Classification

Vendors are classified according to the level of risk they introduce to the organization.

| Risk Level | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Critical | Vendor has access to core banking systems or sensitive financial data |
| High | Vendor processes customer information or supports critical infrastructure |
| Medium | Vendor provides operational services with limited system access |
| Low | Vendor provides services with minimal data or system access |

Higher-risk vendors require more stringent security oversight.

---

# 6. Vendor Security Due Diligence

NovaTrust Bank performs security due diligence before onboarding new vendors.

Due diligence activities include:

• security questionnaires and risk assessments  
• review of vendor security certifications (e.g., ISO 27001)  
• evaluation of vendor incident response capabilities  
• review of data protection and encryption practices

These evaluations help determine whether a vendor meets NovaTrust Bank's security expectations.

---

# 7. Contractual Security Requirements

Vendor agreements include security requirements to ensure that vendors maintain appropriate cybersecurity protections.

Security clauses typically include:

• data protection requirements  
• breach notification obligations  
• access control standards  
• regulatory compliance requirements  
• security audit rights

These contractual provisions ensure that vendors remain accountable for maintaining strong security practices.

---

# 8. Ongoing Vendor Monitoring

Third-party risk management continues throughout the vendor relationship.

NovaTrust Bank performs ongoing monitoring of vendor security posture through:

• periodic security reviews  
• risk reassessment of vendors  
• monitoring of vendor security incidents  
• review of security compliance reports

Continuous monitoring ensures that vendor security risks remain under control.

---

# 9. Third-Party Access Management

Vendors requiring system access must follow strict access management controls.

Controls include:

• role-based access control for vendor accounts  
• multi-factor authentication for vendor access  
• monitoring of vendor system activity  
• periodic review of vendor access permissions

These controls ensure that vendor access remains tightly controlled.

---

# 10. Vendor Incident Management

If a third-party vendor experiences a security incident, NovaTrust Bank must be notified promptly.

Vendor incident procedures include:

• immediate notification of security breaches  
• coordination of incident investigation  
• assessment of potential impact on banking systems  
• mitigation of security risks

This ensures that third-party incidents do not escalate into major security events.

---

# 11. Regulatory Compliance for Third-Party Risk

Financial regulators require banks to manage third-party cybersecurity risks carefully.

NovaTrust Bank ensures compliance with regulatory expectations by:

• maintaining vendor risk registers  
• performing vendor security assessments  
• monitoring vendor security controls  
• documenting vendor risk management activities

These practices ensure compliance with financial sector regulations.

---

# 12. Continuous Improvement of Third-Party Risk Management

Cyber risks within supply chains evolve as new technologies and vendors emerge.

NovaTrust Bank continuously improves its TPRM program through:

• periodic review of vendor risk policies  
• updates to vendor security assessment processes  
• adoption of advanced vendor monitoring tools

Continuous improvement ensures that vendor security risks remain effectively managed.

---

# 13. Strategic Importance of Third-Party Risk Management

Effective third-party risk management protects NovaTrust Bank from supply chain cyber threats.

By implementing strong vendor security governance, NovaTrust Bank can:

• reduce supply chain cyber risk  
• protect customer financial data  
• maintain operational resilience  
• strengthen regulatory compliance

This approach ensures that third-party partnerships remain secure and aligned with the bank's cybersecurity standards.
