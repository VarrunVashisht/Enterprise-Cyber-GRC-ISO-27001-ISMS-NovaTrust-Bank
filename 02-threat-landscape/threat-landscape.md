# NovaTrust Bank
## Cybersecurity Governance Program
### Module 02 – Financial Sector Threat Landscape

---

# 1. Introduction

Financial institutions are among the most heavily targeted organizations in the global cyber threat landscape. Banks process high-value financial transactions and store sensitive customer data, making them attractive targets for cybercriminal groups, organized fraud networks, and nation-state actors.

NovaTrust Bank must continuously monitor the evolving cyber threat environment to ensure that cybersecurity controls effectively address emerging risks.

Understanding the financial sector threat landscape enables NovaTrust Bank to:

• Identify relevant cyber threats  
• Prioritize cybersecurity investments  
• Strengthen defensive controls  
• Improve incident response capabilities  

---

# 2. Major Threat Actor Categories

Cyber threats targeting financial institutions originate from several types of threat actors. Each actor has different motivations and attack methods.

## 2.1 Financial Cybercrime Groups

Financial cybercrime groups are the most common threat to banks. These groups are typically motivated by financial gain and specialize in attacks that enable direct theft of funds or monetization of stolen data.

Common activities include:

• Ransomware attacks targeting banking infrastructure  
• Credential theft through phishing campaigns  
• Payment fraud and account takeover  
• Malware used to compromise banking systems  

Examples of known financial cybercrime groups include ransomware operations and organized cybercriminal networks targeting global banking institutions.

---

## 2.2 Nation-State Threat Actors

Nation-state actors are advanced cyber groups sponsored by governments. These groups may target financial institutions for strategic intelligence, economic disruption, or financial espionage.

Nation-state attackers typically possess advanced capabilities including:

• Sophisticated malware development  
• Long-term infiltration of networks  
• Exploitation of zero-day vulnerabilities  

Financial institutions may be targeted due to their role in national economies and financial systems.

---

## 2.3 Insider Threats

Insider threats originate from individuals who have legitimate access to organizational systems.

Examples include:

• Employees abusing privileged access  
• Negligent staff exposing sensitive information  
• Disgruntled employees leaking confidential data  

Insider threats can be particularly damaging because attackers may already have authorized system access.

---

## 2.4 Supply Chain Attackers

Modern financial institutions rely heavily on third-party vendors and service providers.

Supply chain attackers exploit vulnerabilities in vendor systems to gain indirect access to banking infrastructure.

Examples include:

• Compromised software updates from vendors  
• Vulnerabilities in cloud service providers  
• Weak security controls at third-party partners  

Supply chain attacks can bypass traditional security defenses if vendor security is not properly managed.

---

# 3. Common Cyber Attack Methods

Threat actors targeting banks frequently use a combination of technical and social engineering techniques.

## 3.1 Phishing and Credential Theft

Phishing remains one of the most common attack vectors targeting financial institutions.

Attackers send fraudulent emails or messages designed to trick employees into revealing login credentials or downloading malware.

Successful phishing attacks can lead to:

• Unauthorized system access  
• Account takeover  
• Internal network compromise  

---

## 3.2 Ransomware Attacks

Ransomware attacks involve malicious software that encrypts organizational systems and demands payment for data recovery.

Banks are prime ransomware targets because service disruption can severely impact financial operations.

Ransomware attacks may result in:

• Service outages  
• Data loss  
• Financial extortion  
• Reputational damage  

---

## 3.3 Payment Fraud

Payment fraud attacks attempt to manipulate banking systems or processes to transfer funds illegally.

Examples include:

• fraudulent payment instructions  
• business email compromise  
• unauthorized fund transfers  

Payment fraud represents a significant risk to financial institutions due to the direct financial impact.

---

## 3.4 Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS)

DDoS attacks overwhelm banking systems with traffic to disrupt services.

Such attacks may cause:

• online banking outages  
• disruption of payment processing  
• reduced customer service availability  

DDoS attacks are sometimes used as distractions for more complex cyber attacks.

---

## 3.5 Malware and Banking Trojans

Malware specifically designed for financial institutions may be used to steal credentials, monitor transactions, or manipulate banking systems.

Examples include:

• banking trojans targeting online banking users  
• malware used to capture login credentials  
• remote access tools used to maintain attacker access  

---

# 4. Emerging Threat Trends

The financial cyber threat landscape continues to evolve rapidly.

Key emerging threats include:

## 4.1 Cloud Infrastructure Attacks

Banks increasingly rely on cloud services, which introduces new attack surfaces such as misconfigured cloud storage or insecure APIs.

## 4.2 AI-Assisted Fraud

Artificial intelligence tools are increasingly being used by attackers to generate convincing phishing emails, deepfake voices, and automated fraud campaigns.

## 4.3 API Exploitation

Modern digital banking platforms rely heavily on APIs to integrate services. Poorly secured APIs can expose sensitive financial data.

---

# 5. Financial Sector Threat Impact

Cyber attacks against banks may have severe consequences.

Potential impacts include:

• financial losses from fraud  
• disruption of banking services  
• exposure of customer financial data  
• regulatory penalties  
• reputational damage  

Because financial institutions are critical to national economies, cyber incidents may also attract regulatory and governmental scrutiny.

---

# 6. Strategic Threat Awareness

NovaTrust Bank must maintain strong cyber threat awareness to proactively address emerging risks.

Effective threat awareness includes:

• monitoring global cyber threat intelligence  
• participating in financial sector threat sharing networks  
• analyzing security incident trends  
• continuously updating defensive strategies  

This threat intelligence informs NovaTrust Bank's enterprise risk assessment and cybersecurity control implementation.
