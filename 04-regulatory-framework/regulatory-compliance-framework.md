# NovaTrust Bank
## Cybersecurity Governance Program
### Module 04 – Regulatory & Compliance Framework

---

# 1. Introduction

Financial institutions operate within one of the most highly regulated sectors of the global economy. Cybersecurity governance within NovaTrust Bank must therefore align with regulatory requirements, industry standards, and legal obligations that protect financial systems and customer data.

This regulatory and compliance framework establishes the standards, regulations, and security obligations that guide NovaTrust Bank's cybersecurity program.

By aligning cybersecurity controls with recognized regulatory frameworks, NovaTrust Bank ensures that digital banking services remain secure, resilient, and compliant with financial supervisory expectations.

---

# 2. Regulatory Environment for Financial Institutions

Banks operate within a complex regulatory environment designed to maintain financial stability and protect customer assets.

Cybersecurity governance must therefore address multiple regulatory domains including:

• financial system stability  
• protection of customer financial data  
• operational resilience  
• fraud prevention  
• privacy protection  

Regulators expect financial institutions to demonstrate strong cyber risk management, incident response capabilities, and operational resilience.

---

# 3. Key Cybersecurity Regulatory Requirements

NovaTrust Bank aligns its cybersecurity program with several regulatory frameworks that govern financial institutions.

These frameworks define minimum security requirements for protecting financial infrastructure and customer data.

---

# 4. APRA CPS 234 – Information Security

The Australian Prudential Regulation Authority (APRA) CPS 234 standard establishes information security requirements for financial institutions operating in Australia.

The objective of CPS 234 is to ensure that regulated entities maintain robust information security capabilities commensurate with the threats they face.

Key requirements include:

• maintaining information security capabilities  
• identifying and managing information security risks  
• implementing appropriate security controls  
• ensuring third-party service provider security  
• notifying regulators of material security incidents

NovaTrust Bank aligns its cybersecurity governance framework with the requirements defined under CPS 234 to ensure compliance with Australian financial regulations.

---

# 5. ISO/IEC 27001:2022 – Information Security Management System

NovaTrust Bank implements its cybersecurity governance program based on the ISO/IEC 27001:2022 standard.

ISO 27001 provides a structured Information Security Management System (ISMS) that enables organizations to systematically manage information security risks.

Key elements of the ISO 27001 framework include:

• risk-based security management  
• security governance and leadership oversight  
• continuous monitoring and improvement  
• implementation of security controls across people, processes, and technology

Adopting ISO 27001 enables NovaTrust Bank to maintain internationally recognized security governance practices.

---

# 6. NIST Cybersecurity Framework

The NIST Cybersecurity Framework provides a risk-based approach for managing cybersecurity risks across critical infrastructure sectors, including financial services.

The framework is organized into five core functions:

| Function | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Identify | Understand cybersecurity risks and assets |
| Protect | Implement safeguards to protect systems |
| Detect | Identify cybersecurity events and anomalies |
| Respond | Manage and contain security incidents |
| Recover | Restore services after cybersecurity events |

NovaTrust Bank uses the NIST Cybersecurity Framework to structure operational cybersecurity activities and enhance cyber resilience.

---

# 7. Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard (PCI DSS)

NovaTrust Bank processes payment card transactions through digital banking systems and payment gateways.

The Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard (PCI DSS) defines security requirements for organizations that process, store, or transmit payment card information.

Key PCI DSS security requirements include:

• protecting cardholder data  
• implementing strong access controls  
• monitoring and testing networks  
• maintaining secure systems and applications

Compliance with PCI DSS ensures the protection of payment card information across NovaTrust Bank's digital banking infrastructure.

---

# 8. Privacy and Data Protection Regulations

Financial institutions are responsible for protecting customer personal data in accordance with privacy regulations.

NovaTrust Bank complies with relevant privacy laws including:

• Australian Privacy Act  
• international data protection obligations where applicable

These regulations require organizations to:

• collect personal data lawfully  
• protect personal information from unauthorized access  
• notify regulators and customers in case of data breaches  
• ensure responsible data handling practices

Protecting customer personal data is a critical component of NovaTrust Bank's cybersecurity governance program.

---

# 9. Regulatory Reporting and Incident Notification

Financial regulators require banks to report significant cybersecurity incidents that could impact financial systems or customer data.

NovaTrust Bank maintains procedures to ensure timely reporting of material security incidents to relevant regulatory authorities.

Reporting obligations include:

• notification of major cyber incidents  
• reporting of operational disruptions  
• disclosure of material data breaches

These procedures ensure transparency and regulatory compliance during cybersecurity events.

The regulatory and compliance framework is integrated into NovaTrust Bank's cybersecurity governance program.

Security policies, risk assessments, and operational controls are designed to support compliance with regulatory requirements.

Compliance monitoring includes:

This integrated approach ensures that NovaTrust Bank maintains strong cybersecurity governance while meeting financial sector regulatory expectations.
